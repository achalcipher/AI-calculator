# Unit Economics and Profitability

If Credex deployed tomorrow, our viability hinges on converting a free, frictionless audit into high-value consulting contracts or software credits. Here is the mathematical breakdown of our unit economics and the explicit path to $1M ARR.

## Expected Value of a Converted Lead
Our core monetization is a "SaaS Consolidation Consultation," billed at a flat fee of $1,500. During this engagement, we negotiate and consolidate their AI stack.
- **Consultation Price:** $1,500
- **Close Rate:** We estimate 15% of qualified leads (who submitted their email after seeing >$1k in savings) will sign the contract.
- **Lead Value (EV):** $1,500 * 0.15 = **$225 per captured lead**.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel

| Channel | Cost Input | Conversion (Visitor -> Lead) | Cost Per Lead (CPL) | CAC (Cost to get 1 Client) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Manual Outbound (LinkedIn)** | $0 (Sweat Equity) | 10% | $0.00 | **$0.00** |
| **Community Posts (Slack)** | $0 (Sweat Equity) | 15% | $0.00 | **$0.00** |
| **LinkedIn Ads (Future)** | $6.00 / Click | 8% | $75.00 | **$500.00** |
| **Search Ads (Google)** | $4.00 / Click | 12% | $33.33 | **$222.20** |

*Note: CAC = CPL / 0.15 (our 15% close rate).*

## The Conversion Funnel
To be profitable, our funnel must maintain strict minimum conversion thresholds at each stage:
1. **Visitor to Audit Completed:** 30% (High, due to no email gate).
2. **Audit Completed to Lead Captured (Consultation Booked):** 5% (Only a fraction will have enough waste to justify giving us their email).
3. **Lead Captured to Contract Signed (Credit Purchase):** 15%.

*Funnel Math:* For every 1,000 visitors, we get 300 completed audits, 15 qualified leads, and 2.25 paying clients ($3,375 in revenue).

## The Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months
To hit $1M ARR solely on $1,500 consulting contracts, we need to close **667 deals per year** (about 55 deals per month).
Using our funnel math, here is exactly what must be true to sustain a $1M ARR run rate by Month 18:

| Metric | Monthly Target | Annual Equivalent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Target Revenue** | $83,333 | $1,000,000 |
| **Clients Needed (at $1,500)** | ~56 | 667 |
| **Leads Needed (at 15% close)** | 373 | 4,476 |
| **Audits Completed (at 5% lead conv.)** | 7,460 | 89,520 |
| **Unique Visitors (at 30% audit conv.)** | 24,866 | 298,392 |

### What has to be true?
To drive ~25,000 unique visitors a month, we cannot rely solely on manual outbound. 
By Month 6, we must transition to paid acquisition. Assuming a blended CAC of $350 (Google + LinkedIn ads):
- We will spend **$19,600 / month** in ad spend to acquire those 56 clients.
- 56 clients * $1,500 = **$84,000 / month gross revenue**.
- **Net Margin:** $84,000 - $19,600 = **$64,400 / month**.
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** $1,500 / $350 = **4.2x**.

This 4.2x ratio proves that as long as we maintain a 15% close rate on the leads generated, scaling to $1M ARR is purely a matter of deploying ad capital efficiently.
