# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## Exact Target User
Our exact target user is the "Director of RevOps" or "Head of Finance" at Series B B2B SaaS companies (roughly 100-250 employees). At this stage, they have just raised $20M-$50M, scaled their headcount aggressively, and handed out corporate cards without strict oversight. Now, the board is demanding a path to profitability, and they are tasked with cutting software bloat.

## High-Intent Search Queries
Right before they realize they need our tool, they are actively Googling or searching internal Wikis for:
- "How to track shadow IT spend across engineering teams"
- "Average software spend per employee Series B"
- "Cursor Enterprise vs Pro security features necessary?"
- "Automated SaaS vendor consolidation tools"

## Where They Hang Out Online
These operators do not hang out on Twitter or generic forums. They are active in highly specialized, gated communities where they ask peers for vendor recommendations:
- **Slack Communities:** RevOps Co-op, CFO Connect, and the Pavilion Executive Slack.
- **Subreddits:** r/SaaS (for broader startup operations) and r/FinancialCareers (niche FP&A threads).
- **Newsletters:** They read "The SaaS CFO" by Ben Murray and "Gergely Orosz's Pragmatic Engineer" (specifically the editions discussing engineering tool budgets).

## Acquiring the First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
We will bypass generic content marketing and execute a highly targeted, manual outbound sprint.

1. **The "Tear-Down" Outbound Campaign (Weeks 1-2):** I will scrape Crunchbase for SaaS companies that raised a Series A or B exactly 18 months ago (they are now feeling the burn rate pressure). I will find their Head of Finance on LinkedIn. I will send a personalized DM with a pre-filled, customized audit URL: *"Hey [Name], noticed [Company] scaled fast since the Series B. Most teams your size waste ~$15k on redundant Cursor/Claude enterprise seats. I built a free tool that flags this. I ran a mock audit based on your 50-person engineering team: [Link]. No email required to see the math."*
2. **The Community "Trojan Horse" (Weeks 3-4):** I will join RevOps Co-op and CFO Connect. I won't pitch the product. Instead, I will post an overly detailed, spreadsheet-style breakdown of how my last company saved $30,000 downgrading AI tools. At the very bottom, I will link to the Credex calculator: *"If you want to run this math for your own team without the spreadsheet headache, I turned my formula into a free calculator here."*

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
My unfair advantage is leveraging the actual end-users (engineers) to sell to the decision-makers (Finance). By making the audit free and instantly shareable, an Engineering Manager who realizes they are over budget can instantly generate a beautiful, anonymous link and drop it into their Slack channel. We aren't doing cold sales; we are arming internal champions with data to sell their CFOs for us.

## Week-1 Traction Expectations
If this strategy hits, Week 1 looks like this:
- **150 Unique Page Views** (driven primarily by the LinkedIn tear-down campaign).
- **45 Audits Completed** (30% conversion rate from view to audit, due to zero friction).
- **12 "Share Anonymous Audit" Links Generated** (proving the viral loop works).
- **4 Qualified Leads Captured** (users who have >$1,000 in savings and actually enter their email for a consultation).
